How I got here

The Architect

An exact date would be weird

It all started with my uncle. Whether it be staring at his original Wassily chairs or parking myself in his library stacked with books about topics ranging from Bauhaus to Leicas, there was always something new to marvel at, and learn about. I'd probably be a dentist (shoutout to dentists though) if it wasn't for my uncle.

My uncle taught me that design is universal, and shapes the world around us..

Passing Through

2013-Present

Since middle school, when I got clowned for wearing his brand, Bobby Hundreds been a hero to me. Besides acting counter to the socially-constructed template of how to look or act, he taught me the importance of making work that makes people feel excited, special, and part of a community.

Bobby taught me that design should be fun, socially and identity driven, and for the people.

The Paella of Destiny

2017

After consuming too much paella and fideua at a friend's home somewhere in Barcelona, a cheery-eyed Brendan misclicked on the poorly-designed university portal and accidentally enrolled into a design program rather than sciences (unnamed sources say his parents are still mad).

This (awkwardly) introduced me to the importance of user experience design.
