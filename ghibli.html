Unpacking the Studio Ghibli Brand

When leading strategy and UX research for this project, it as determined we should unpack Studio Ghibli’s brand pillars as a means to understand their market positioning and how they provide unique value for varying consumers. We’ve identified three main pillars of: Purpose, perception, and position.

Opportunity: No Clue

The digital touchpoints to Studio Ghibli are scattered across multiple third-party sites, many of which are only offered in Japanese. This frustrates consumers when trying to find reliable content direct from the studio—leaving the consumer disjointed with a poor impression of the Studio Ghibli brand.

Strategy: Narrowing Our Scope

Our aspirations for this project were lofty and unrealistic. We followed assumptions which brought us into creating band-aid solutions. I refined our scope to address two primary business and consumer needs defined from earlier rounds of primary and secondary research. These require minimal invesetment while resulting in meaningful long-term engagement.

Intervention: Extending the Journey

Following a series of interview with Ghibli fans, after watching a film, we found viewers often analyze what they’ve watched, or discuss with friends, and it’s at this stage where we proposed to intervene. We want to tap into the relationship that existing customers have with the studio to reignite their interest in Ghibli’s collection of work through further immersion into the brand and its products.

Intervention: Key Entry Points

Existing fans lack engagement with Studio Ghibli beyond their initial interaction with films, or other products. Scattered primary and third-party touchpoints drive a wedge between the business and fans, leading to decreased revenue and an unfavorable perception of brand. How might we improve emotional interpersonal connection by creating a way for workers to communicate in a wordless medium?
